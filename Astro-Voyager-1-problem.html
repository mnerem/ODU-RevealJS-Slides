Voyager 1 Distance Problem

Voyager 1 is travelling at 61,146 km/hr towards a star that is 6.2 light-year away. How many years will it take Voyager 1 to reach its destination?

The distance an object travels is equal to the speed
of the object times the time spent travelling. $$\begin{gathered} \text{distance}=(\text{velocity})\times(\text{time}) \quad \Rightarrow \quad d=vt\end{gathered}$$ We know the distance and the velocity, so we can solve for the time.

Voyager 1 Distance Problem

$$\begin{gathered} t=\frac{d}{v} \\ \notag \text{plug in the values we know to get $\ldots$} \\ t=\frac{6.2\text{light-year}}{61,146\,\text{km/hr}} = 1.014\times10^{-4}\,\frac{\text{light-year}}{\text{km}}\text{hr}\end{gathered}$$ While technically correct, this answer is difficult to understand because the units are strange. There are a couple of routes to go about fixing this.

A recommend strategy is to convert the distance in light-years to kilometers so that the light-year unit cancels out with the kilometer unit from the velocity. $$d=6.2\,\text{light-year}\times \left(\frac{9.461\times10^{12}\,\text{km}}{1\,\text{light-year}} \right) =5.866\times10^{13}\,\text{km}$$

Voyager 1 Distance Problem

Now calculate the time to get there. $$\begin{gathered} t=\frac{d}{v}=\frac{5.866\times10^{13}\,\text{km}}{61,146\,\text{km/hr}} =9.593\times10^{8}\,\text{hr}\end{gathered}$$ Now we know how much time it will take Voyager 1 in hours to get there!
Lets convert hours to years to get our final answer. $$\begin{gathered} t=9.593\times10^{8}\,\text{hr}\times \left( \frac{1\,\text{day}}{24\,\text{hr}}\right) \times \left( \frac{1\,\text{year}}{365.25\,\text{day}}\right) =1.094\times10^{5}\,\text{year}\end{gathered}$$ It will take Voyager one about 109 thousand years to reach the star.
